\chapter{Conclusion and Future work}

So, what did we learn? We achieved a massive 356.2x speedup, but did we sacrifice quality? Absolutely not! In fact, the OptiX thickness maps look significantly better, smoother, and more realistic than the PyMeshLab results! 

Why? Because PyMeshLab just spits out raw, noisy numbers. Our OptiX pipeline uses the clever "dimmer-switch" smoothing filter to clean up the noise while perfectly preserving sharp edges. This project proves that using dedicated ray tracing hardware (OptiX) isn't just faster—it's fundamentally better!

Moving forward, there are several exciting avenues for future work. First, the current pipeline could be extended to support dynamic, animated meshes by updating the BVH in real-time. Second, we plan to explore advanced filtering techniques, such as AI-driven denoising, to further enhance the quality of the SDF heatmaps without increasing the ray count. Finally, integrating this OptiX-based thickness calculation directly into popular 3D modeling software as a plug-in would make this powerful tool accessible to a much broader audience of artists and engineers.
